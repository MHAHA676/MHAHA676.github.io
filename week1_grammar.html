  1. N1 は N2 です
    The particle は indicates that the noun before it (N1) is the topic of the sentence.
    Noun used with です works as predicates. Besides indicating judgement or assertion, です also shows politeness towards the listener.
  2. N1 は N2 では(じゃ)ありません
    ではありません is the negative form of です. It is often used in formal speech and writing. While in daily conversation, we can use じゃありません.
  3. N1 は N2 ですか
    The particle か is used to express uncertainty,doubt,etc. on the part of speaker. A question can be formed simply by adding か to the end of the sentence. A question usually ends in a rising intonation.
  4. N も
    [も] is used when the same thing applies as was stated previously.
  5. N1 の N2
    When a noun N1 modifies a following noun N2, の is used to connect the two nouns. Eg. N2 belongs to N1.
